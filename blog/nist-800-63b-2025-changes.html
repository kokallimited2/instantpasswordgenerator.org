📋 NIST SP 800-63B: What Changed in 2025 and How It Affects Your Policy

By Alex Chen · 2 May 2026 · 6 min read

Category: Security Standards Author: Alex Chen Published: 2 May 2026 Reading time: 6 min read

In this guide

  1. Key Changes

The July 2025 update to NIST SP 800-63B finalised long-awaited guidance. If your policy hasn't been updated since 2024, it's likely out of step with current best practice.

Key Changes

  • Mandatory rotation is out: Forced periodic changes are now explicitly discouraged unless there's evidence of compromise.
  • Minimum length: 15 characters recommended for accounts without MFA.
  • Maximum must be 64+: Systems must accept passwords up to at least 64 characters.
  • No composition rules: Mandating specific character types is discouraged. Check against breach databases instead.
